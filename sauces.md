# Mother Sauces

The five French mother sauces form the foundation of classical cooking. Each can be extended into dozens of derivative sauces.

---

## 1. Béchamel (White Sauce)

**Base:** Milk thickened with white roux

### Ingredients
- Whole milk
- Butter
- All-purpose flour
- Salt, white pepper, nutmeg

### Roux-to-Liquid Ratios

| Sauce Consistency | Butter | Flour  | Milk   |
|-------------------|--------|--------|--------|
| Thin (soup)       | 15 g   | 15 g   | 500 ml |
| Medium (coating)  | 30 g   | 30 g   | 500 ml |
| Thick (soufflé)   | 60 g   | 60 g   | 500 ml |

### Method
1. Melt butter over medium heat; whisk in flour. Cook 2 min (no colour).
2. Gradually whisk in warm milk. Simmer 10 min, stirring constantly.

---

## 2. Velouté (Blonde Sauce)

**Base:** Light white stock thickened with blonde roux

### Ingredients
- Chicken, veal, or fish stock
- Butter
- All-purpose flour

### Roux-to-Stock Ratios

| Sauce Consistency | Butter | Flour  | Stock  |
|-------------------|--------|--------|--------|
| Thin (soup)       | 15 g   | 15 g   | 500 ml |
| Medium (coating)  | 30 g   | 30 g   | 500 ml |
| Thick (binding)   | 60 g   | 60 g   | 500 ml |

### Method
1. Make a blonde roux (cook until pale golden, ~3–4 min).
2. Whisk in hot stock gradually. Simmer 20 min, skimming as needed.

---

## 3. Espagnole (Brown Sauce)

**Base:** Brown beef/veal stock thickened with brown roux + tomato paste

### Ingredients
- Brown veal or beef stock
- Butter
- All-purpose flour
- Tomato paste (1 tbsp per 500 ml stock)
- Mirepoix (onion, carrot, celery)

### Roux-to-Stock Ratios

| Sauce Consistency | Butter | Flour  | Stock  |
|-------------------|--------|--------|--------|
| Medium (sauce)    | 30 g   | 30 g   | 500 ml |

### Method
1. Cook mirepoix in butter until browned. Add tomato paste; cook 2 min.
2. Add dark roux (cooked until nutty brown). Whisk in hot stock.
3. Simmer 1–2 hours, skimming regularly. Strain before use.

---

## 4. Sauce Tomat (Tomato Sauce)

**Base:** Tomatoes + pork or veal stock; may use a light roux as optional thickener

### Ingredients
- Ripe tomatoes (or canned, ~800 g per 500 ml sauce)
- Onion, garlic, carrot, celery
- Butter or olive oil
- Light stock (optional)
- Salt, pepper, bay leaf, thyme

### Optional Roux-to-Stock Ratios (if thickening with roux)

| Use               | Butter | Flour  | Stock  |
|-------------------|--------|--------|--------|
| Light thickening  | 15 g   | 15 g   | 250 ml |

### Method
1. Sweat aromatics in fat. Add tomatoes and stock.
2. Simmer 45–60 min. Blend and strain (or leave rustic).

---

## 5. Hollandaise (Emulsified Butter Sauce)

**Base:** Egg yolk emulsion with clarified butter; no roux

### Ingredients
- Egg yolks (3 yolks per 250 ml clarified butter)
- Clarified butter, warm
- Lemon juice or white wine reduction
- Salt, white pepper, cayenne

### Ratios

| Yield     | Egg Yolks | Clarified Butter | Acid Reduction |
|-----------|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| ~250 ml   | 3         | 250 ml           | 2 tbsp         |
| ~500 ml   | 6         | 500 ml           | 4 tbsp         |

### Method
1. Whisk yolks with acid reduction over a bain-marie until ribbon-thick.
2. Remove from heat; drizzle in warm clarified butter while whisking.
3. Season. Serve immediately or hold at 60°C / 140°F.

## Roux Summary

| Roux Type | Cook Time | Colour       | Used In              |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| White     | 2 min     | Pale/white   | Béchamel             |
| Blonde    | 3–4 min   | Pale golden  | Velouté              |
| Brown     | 8–10 min  | Nut-brown    | Espagnole            |
| Dark      | 15+ min   | Dark brown   | Cajun/Creole gumbos  |

**Base roux ratio:** equal parts butter and flour by weight (1:1)

**General thickening guide (medium-bodied sauce):**
- 30 g butter + 30 g flour per 500 ml liquid
